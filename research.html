Research Interests

My PhD thesis focused on mesoscopic modeling and simulation of complex fluid flows using lattice Boltzmann method (LBM). Efforts are devoted to investigate gas-liquid two-phase flows, particulate flows, and flows in porous media. It would be much more difficult to complete these simulations without the use of various high performance computing (HPC) techniques, such as OpenMP, MPI, and OpenACC.

After graduation, my research interests also extend to microscopic simulations, such as calculating intrinsic transport properties with molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, and adsorbates on surfaces using density functional theory (DFT) calculation.

Overall, my research philosophy is exploring the physical/chemical systems with various advanced numerical tools.
